About BitAuth and this forum

The Bitcoin Grant Forum is a platform demonstrating the capabilities and features of BitAuth. BitAuth is a system allowing users to authenticate with websites using only their Bitcoin address, with no password required. Users simply sign a temporary token using any major bitcoin client and simply paste in the signature to log in.

Here are some advantages to this system:

  • Security

    Traditional login systems require users to provide a password, which they implicitly trust the online service provider to keep secret. In practice, this often does not happen, and user passwords are regularly stolen from website databases, compromising the user's accounts on any systems where they reused their password or a variation of their password. BitAuth sidesteps this problem by storing no non-public user information on the server - only usernames and publicly visible Bitcoin addresses are stored.

  • Unification of financial and online identity, without sacrificing anonymity

    Users of services such as this forum which implement BitAuth can reap the benefits of financial interaction facilitated by conversation. Tips for good advice and funding for open source projects are only some of the possibilities.
